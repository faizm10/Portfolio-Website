14:T1025,<h2>A lightbulb moment in CIS*3750</h2>
<p>Ever spent way too long hunting down the perfect professor for a course, or refreshing Reddit threads for difficulty ratings? That was me last year—scrolling through 2024 outlines, pestering upper years, and waiting on Discord notifications. Course selection shouldn’t feel like blindfolded pinball, but that’s exactly how choosing classes at Guelph felt.</p>
<p>One afternoon in CIS*3750, I was venting to Harkirat about this mess of links and PDFs. We sketched out the idea on a whiteboard: what if there was one place to <strong>browse</strong> every course, <strong>filter</strong> by term or professor, and read <strong>real student reviews</strong>? I hit up Michael and he was down to help us tackle data scraping. In minutes, we had the core concept—uoguelph.courses was officially born.</p>
<h2>Defining a clear goal</h2>
<p>We didn’t need a flashy launch—that would only slow us down. Our mission was simple:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Build a no-nonsense course search that actually helps UofG students plan their semesters.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Key features at launch:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fast course search by title or code</li>
<li>Filters for term, department, and difficulty</li>
<li>Professor profiles with past teaching history</li>
<li>Student reviews (guarded by Google-only login)</li>
<li>Clean, responsive UI in Next.js + Tailwind</li>
</ul>
<p>We looked at sites like:
<img src="/content/uwflow.png" alt="uwflow.com"><br>
<img src="/content/mcgill.png" alt="mcgill.courses"></p>
<p>—and thought, “Okay, something like this—but built for Guelph.”</p>
<h2>From sketch to site</h2>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Frontend groundwork</strong><br>
Harkirat and I set up the Next.js pages, crafted layouts with Tailwind, and wired in basic search and filter components.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Data gathering</strong><br>
Michael built Python scrapers to pull course listings from WebAdvisor and ratings from RateMyProfessors. Suddenly, we had hundreds of courses and professor records ready to display.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>UI polish &#x26; integration</strong><br>
I merged course and professor data into unified cards—now you could see who taught CS*3760 in Fall 2023 alongside real student feedback, all in one glance.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Authentication &#x26; reviews</strong><br>
Talha stepped in to design our Supabase schema and add Google-account login. That way, reviews came from actual students, not anonymous bots.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>In four weeks, we went from blank repo to a working v1:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Search</strong> by code/title</li>
<li><strong>Filter</strong> by term &#x26; department</li>
<li><strong>Course cards</strong> with offerings, instructors, and average ratings</li>
<li><strong>Login-gated</strong> review system</li>
<li><strong>Feedback form</strong> for future ideas</li>
</ul>
<h2>Early reactions</h2>
<p>We dropped the link in Discord and Reddit, fully expecting a polite nod and crickets. Instead, students dove in:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“This saved me hours—thank you!”<br>
“Finally, something that feels built for Guelph.”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In the first week alone, the site logged <strong>1.5 k unique visitors</strong>, <strong>6 k pageviews</strong>, and an estimated <strong>20–25 k</strong> total reach across social shares:</p>
<p><img src="/content/launch.png" alt="launch stats"></p>
<h2>What’s on deck</h2>
<p>With the core live and feedback rolling in, we’re already planning the next features:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>GPA calculator</strong> for planning course loads</li>
<li><strong>Schedule builder</strong> to spot timetable conflicts</li>
<li><strong>Advanced filters</strong> by difficulty, breadth requirement, and interest tags</li>
<li><strong>Degree planner</strong> to track progress toward graduation</li>
<li><strong>Course alerts</strong> for enrollment openings</li>
</ul>
<p>uoguelph.courses isn’t done—it’s only getting started. If you’re a Guelph student and haven’t tried it yet, give it a spin and let us know what you need next.</p>
