## 2. scope

you can talk to me about <UL>anything</UL>.

most of the time it will land somewhere in these buckets, but nothing is off limits. this is not reporting back and forth like a manager. i am just another guy who wants to see people succeed.

1. <HL tone="green">strategy</HL>: picking the right problems, defining the goal, and reducing noise.
2. <HL tone="green">execution</HL>: turning ideas into shipped work, with accountability and next steps.
3. <HL tone="green">career</HL>: resumes, interviews, offers, pivots, and leverage.
4. <HL tone="green">systems</HL>: planning, decision making, and building repeatable habits.

mentor is not a therapist, emergency hotline, coding bootcamp, or motivation podcast. that is not the vibe. this is for strategy, accountability, execution, and results.

the way you get the most out of this is simple. take <HL tone="yellow">advantage</HL> of the access. ask hard questions. bring real problems. tell the truth. then go do something with it.

## 3. timeline

the timeline is <UL>mutually agreed</UL> if you choose to sign. we will set a start date and a cadence that fits your schedule and goals.

if we need to change dates, we talk, we agree, and we keep moving.

## 4. pricing & payment

this is <HL tone="yellow">free</HL>.

i am not charging because i want to make sure my mentee gets the best learning experience possible, with real support and real standards. the cost is not money. the cost is effort, honesty, and follow through.

1. show up.
2. do the work.
3. keep me updated.

## 5. where to find me

you know where to find me.

if you do not, that is already a skill issue, and we should probably start there.

## 6. intellectual property

anything you share with me stays yours. your ideas, drafts, private context, and personal strategy are not for the group chat.

mentor may reuse general learnings and techniques that are not specific to you and do not reveal sensitive details.

## 7. confidentiality

both parties agree to keep shared information confidential, except where disclosure is required by law.

confidential includes, but is not limited to:

1. startup ideas and product direction
2. career strategies, offers, and negotiation details
3. any message that starts with <HL tone="gray">don’t share this yet</HL>

## 8. termination

either of us can end this whenever. no hard feelings.

if you succeed, you are good to be a <UL>superstar</UL>.

if you fail to communicate, give no updates, and disappear, good luck. this only works if you actually use it.

## 9. acceptance

by signing, both parties agree to the terms above.

mentor is not liable for outcomes caused by hesitation, overthinking, or ignoring good advice. growth pains are part of the process.

## 12. governing principles

these are non negotiable.

1. <UL>just ship</UL>. execution is the standard.
2. <UL>builder mentality</UL>. create, iterate, improve real things.
3. <UL>real world impact</UL>. solve actual problems and create measurable value.
4. <UL>ownership over excuses</UL>. outcomes are owned, good or bad.
5. <UL>have a life outside coding</UL>. sustainability is part of winning.
6. <UL>momentum over perfection</UL>. consistent progress beats occasional bursts.
